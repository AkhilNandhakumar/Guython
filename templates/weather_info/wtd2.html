When it's Cold:

This weather phenomena is common and periodic (Every winter for the Northern Hemisphere), as the temperature of the earth is dynamic and changes depending on where and when you are.Freezing temperatures are characterized by the appearance of frost or snow in the air. Temperatures like this may include strong winds and heavy hail, and snow.

What to do:

What NOT do to:

  • Stay inside: No risk of cold or lightning
  • Wear insulating clothing: Jackets, hoodies, sweaters, etc.
  • Turn on electronics: The warmth generate by a heater escpecially in an enclosed environment will help insulate you from the frigid temperatures outside
  • Seek shelter: Avoid cold ares, bodies of cold water, and objects that absorb heat
  • Stay near metal objects: Metals conduct heat very well, this will easily freeze you or weld your skin to them if you touch them
  • Take shelter in a forest: There is no cover or shelter to prevent nature from freezing you
  • Go outside: Increases chance of getting hypothermia or hazardous cold
