What are the different types of clouds and how are they classified?

Clouds are classified by the height at which they are in the Earth's atmosphere. High clouds are clouds that are 10,000 to 60,000 feet high. High clouds include cirrus, cirrostratus or cirrocumulus clouds. Mid level clouds are 6,000 to 25,000 feet high, and include altocumulus, altostratus, and nimbostratus clouds. Low clouds are 6,500 feet or lower. Some types of low clouds are cumulus, stratus, cumulonimbus, and stratocumulus.
