When there's a thunderstorm:

Thunderstorms are characterized by the appearance of lightning and thunder throughout the storm. They may include strong winds and heavy rain, and sometimes even snow. These weather phenomena are among the most dangerous ones on earth, as the lightning and other conditions may produce severe damage and loss of life.

What to do:

What NOT do to:

  • Wear insulating clothing: Jackets, hoodies, sweaters, etc.
  • Seek shelter: Avoid elevated areas, bodies of water, and objects that conduct electricity
  • Stay inside: No risk of lightning or cold
  • Turn off electronics: Lightning strike can cause power surges, destroying electronic objects like computers or TVs
  • Go outside: Increases chance of lightning strike or hazardous cold
  • Stay near metal objects: Metal atracts lightning, increasing the chance of being struck
  • Take shelter under trees: Tall trees are more likely to be struck by lightning, and may fall on you
